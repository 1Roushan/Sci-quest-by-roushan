🦖 What Happened to Dinosaurs After the Asteroid Hit?

About 66 million years ago, a giant asteroid (about 10 km wide) struck Earth near what is now the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico. This impact triggered massive wildfires, tsunamis, and a long-lasting "impact winter" that blocked sunlight.

This sudden climate change made it impossible for plants to grow, disrupting the entire food chain. Dinosaurs, who couldn’t adapt quickly, went extinct. However, some small feathered dinosaurs survived — and evolved into modern birds!

So next time you see a sparrow or a crow, just know — they are distant cousins of the mighty T-Rex! 🐦
